CHOLE BHATURE 

Chole Bhature is a beloved North Indian dish, especially popular in Punjab. It consists of spicy chickpea curry (chole) cooked 
with aromatic spices, served alongside fluffy, deep-fried bread called **bhature**. This hearty and indulgent combination is a
favorite for breakfast, lunch, or special occasions. The rich flavors of the chole perfectly complement the crispy yet soft bhature,
making it a satisfying and iconic Indian meal enjoyed across the country.


List of ingredients:

  • For Chole (Chickpea Curry):
    • 1 cup dried chickpeas (soaked overnight) or 2 cups canned chickpeas
    • 2 medium onions, finely chopped
    • 2 tomatoes, pureed
    • 1 tsp ginger-garlic paste
    • 2-3 green chilies, slit (optional)
    • 2 tbsp oil or ghee
    • 1 tsp cumin seeds
    • 1 bay leaf
    • 1 tsp turmeric powder
    • 1 tsp red chili powder
    • 1 tsp coriander powder
    • 1 tsp garam masala
    • Salt to taste
    • Fresh coriander leaves for garnish
  • For Bhature
    • 2 cups all-purpose flour (maida)
    • 1/4 cup semolina (sooji)
    • 1/2 tsp baking soda
    • 1/2 tsp yogurt
    • 2 tsp oil
    • Water for kneading
    • Oil for deep frying

Steps:

  1. Prepare the Chole: 
    • Drain the soaked chickpeas, add them to a pressure cooker with 3 cups of water and a pinch of salt. 
Cook for 4-5 whistles until the chickpeas are soft. If using canned chickpeas, skip this step.

    • Heat oil or ghee in a pan. Add cumin seeds, bay leaf, cinnamon stick, and cloves. Let them crackle.

    • Add chopped onions and saut√© until golden brown. Stir in the ginger-garlic paste and cook 
until the raw smell disappears.

    • Add the tomato puree and cook until the oil separates from the masala.

    • Stir in turmeric, red chili powder, coriander powder, and cumin powder. Cook for 1-2 minutes.
    
    • Add the boiled chickpeas along with their water. Simmer for 10-15 minutes to let the flavors blend.
    
    • Add garam masala and amchur powder for a tangy finish. Garnish with fresh coriander leaves.

  2. Prepare the Bhature Dough: 
    • In a large bowl, mix all-purpose flour, semolina, baking soda, yogurt, and oil.
        
    • Add a little water at a time and knead into a soft, smooth dough. Cover and let it rest for 1-2 hours.

  3. Roll and Fry the Bhature: 

    • Divide the dough into small portions and roll them into oval or round shapes, about 6-8 inches in diameter.
                    
    • Heat oil in a deep frying pan. Once hot, fry the bhature one at a time until they puff up and turn golden
on both sides. Remove and drain on paper towels.
  4. Serve: 
    • Serve the hot and fluffy bhature with spicy chole. Accompany with sliced onions, lemon wedges, and a side of pickles for an authentic experience.

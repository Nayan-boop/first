RAJMA CHAWAL 

         Rajma Chawal is a classic North Indian comfort food, featuring red kidney beans (rajma) 
     cooked in a flavorful tomato-based gravy spiced with aromatic Indian spices. Served with 
     steamed basmati rice (chawal), this wholesome dish is a staple in many Indian households.
     Its rich, creamy texture and hearty flavors make it both nourishing and satisfying, often 
     enjoyed with a dollop of ghee or yogurt on the side. Perfect for a simple yet soul-warming
     meal, rajma chawal is a true celebration of traditional Indian flavors.


List of ingredients:

  • For Rajma:
    • 1 cup rajma (red kidney beans), soaked overnight
    • 2 medium onions, finely chopped
    • 3 medium tomatoes, pureed
    • 1-2 green chilies, finely chopped
    • 1 tsp ginger-garlic paste
    • 2 tbsp oil or ghee
    • 1 tsp cumin seeds
    • 1 bay leaf
    • 1 tsp turmeric powder
    • 1 tsp red chili powder
    • 1 tsp coriander powder
    • 1 tsp garam masala
    • Salt to taste
    • Fresh coriander leaves for garnish
  • For chawal:
    • 1 cup basmati rice
    • 2 cups water
    • A pinch of salt

Steps:

  1. Cook the Rajma:

    • Drain the soaked rajma and pressure cook with 3 cups of water and a pinch of salt for 4-5 whistles, or until the beans are soft. Set aside.

  2. Prepare the Gravy:

    • Heat oil/ghee in a pan. Add cumin seeds and let them splutter. Add the bay leaf.

    • Saut√© onions until golden brown. Add ginger-garlic paste and green chilies, and cook until fragrant.

    • Add tomato puree and cook until the oil separates from the mixture.

  3. Add Spices and Cook:

    • Stir in turmeric, red chili powder, and coriander powder. Cook for 1-2 minutes.

    • Add the cooked rajma along with its water. Simmer for 15-20 minutes, mashing a few beans for a thicker consistency.

    • Add garam masala and adjust salt. Garnish with fresh coriander leaves.

  4. Cook the rajma:

    • Wash basmati rice thoroughly.

    • Boil 2 cups of water with a pinch of salt. Add the rice, cover, and cook on low heat until the water is absorbed and the rice is fluffy.

  5. Serve:
    • Serve the hot rajma over the steamed rice. Enjoy with a side of onion salad, pickle, or yogurt for a complete meal!

Samosa 

A samosa is a popular savory snack originating from the Indian subcontinent. It is a deep-fried or baked pastry 
with a triangular or conical shape, typically filled with spiced potatoes, peas, and sometimes meat or lentils. 
Known for its crispy exterior and flavorful filling, samosas are enjoyed worldwide, often served with chutneys 
or sauces as a delicious appetizer or street food.


List of ingredients:

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1/4 cup oil or ghee
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 cup water (adjust as needed)
  • 3 medium potatoes (boiled, peeled, and mashed)
  • 1/2 cup green peas (cooked)
  • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 1 teaspoon ginger (grated)
  • 1 green chili (finely chopped)
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon garam masala
  • 1/2 teaspoon red chili powder (optional)
  • 1/2 teaspoon coriander powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon amchur (dry mango powder) or lemon juice
  • Salt to taste
  • 2 tablespoons oil
  • Oil for deep frying

Steps:

  1. Make the Dough: 
    • In a large bowl, mix the flour, salt, and oil/ghee until it has a crumbly texture.

    • Gradually add water and knead to form a stiff, smooth dough. Cover with a damp cloth 
and let it rest for 20-30 minutes.

  2. Prepare the Filling: 
    • Heat oil in a pan and add cumin seeds. Let them splutter.
   
    • Heat oil in a pan and add cumin seeds. Let them splutter.
           
    • Add mashed potatoes, peas, turmeric, garam masala, red chili powder, coriander powder, and salt.
        
    • Mix well and cook for 2-3 minutes. Add amchur or lemon juice, mix, and let it cool.

  3. Assemble the Samosas: 
    • Divide the dough into small balls. Roll each ball into a thin oval or circle. Cut it in half.
               
    • Take one half, form it into a cone by overlapping the edges, and seal with water.
               
    • Fill the cone with the potato mixture, leaving space at the top.
            
    • Seal the open edge by pressing firmly with water.

  4. Fry the Samosas: 
    • Heat oil in a deep pan over medium-low heat.

    • Fry the samosas in batches until golden brown and crispy. Maintain a medium-low temperature to ensure even cooking.
    
    • Remove and drain on paper towels.

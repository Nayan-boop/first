KANDHVI 

            Khandvi is a popular Gujarati snack made from gram flour (besan) and buttermilk, known for its delicate,
melt-in-the-mouth texture. The mixture is cooked and rolled into thin, bite-sized spirals, then garnished 
with a tempering of mustard seeds, sesame seeds, curry leaves, and grated coconut. Light, tangy, and mildly
spiced, khandvi is a perfect snack for any time of the day, showcasing the simplicity and elegance of
Gujarati cuisine.


List of ingredients:

  • 1 cup besan (gram flour)
  • 1 cup yogurt (curd)
  • 2 cups water
  • 1/2 tsp turmeric powder
  • 1/2 tsp ginger-green chili paste (optional)
  • 1/4 tsp asafoetida (hing)
  • Salt to taste
  • 2 tbsp oil
  • 1 tsp mustard seeds
  • 1 tsp sesame seeds
  • 6-8 curry leaves
  • 1-2 green chilies, finely chopped
  • 2 tbsp grated fresh coconut
  • 2 tbsp chopped fresh coriander leaves

Steps:

  1. Prepare the Batter:

    • In a mixing bowl, whisk together besan, yogurt, water, turmeric, ginger-green chili paste, asafoetida, and salt. Ensure there are no lumps.

  2. Cook the Mixture:

    • Transfer the batter to a non-stick pan and cook on medium heat, stirring continuously to prevent lumps.

    • Cook until the mixture thickens and turns smooth, leaving the sides of the pan (approximately 10-12 minutes).
    ]
  3. Spread the Mixture:

    • Quickly spread the cooked mixture thinly onto greased steel plates or a clean countertop using a spatula or the back of a flat spoon. Work fast before it sets.

  4. Roll the Khandvi:

    • Once the mixture cools and sets, cut it into long strips. Gently roll each strip tightly into spirals.

  5. Tempering:

    • Heat oil in a small pan. Add mustard seeds, sesame seeds, curry leaves, and green chilies. Let them splutter.

    • Pour the tempering over the khandvi rolls.

  6. Garnish and Serve:

    • Sprinkle grated coconut and chopped coriander leaves over the rolls.

    • Serve as a light snack or appetizer.
